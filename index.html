Clara A. Sava-Segal




I'm a Cognitive Neuroscience PhD student advised by Emily Finn in the Functional Imaging and Naturalistic Neuroscience Lab at Dartmouth College.


Research interests

I use a combination of neuroimaging and behavior to explore questions such as how can individuals with a shared experience reach variable memories, conclusions or interpretations about the exact same events. I'm also interested in other relevant questions like:

    What is the interaction between past knowledge/memories and incoming sensory information?

    How do we integrate incoming information with our existent knowledge?

    What are the neural dynamics and patterns supporting encoding and recall, and how are these modified and updated with impression changes?

    How can we identify meaningful individual variability?

    How can we experimentally manipulate or perturb these processes?


Relevant background

I got my Bachelor's from the University of Chicago where I completed my thesis with Daniel Casasanto and also was an undergraduate RA in Christopher Gomez's lab and in the Awh-Vogel Lab. I then worked as a lab manager/research assistant at Stanford in Josef Parvizi's lab.

I also am interested in science communication and public education. Prior to graduate school, I did some medical editing and worked in classrooms (specifically, Pre-K and 12th grade, so I got both crazy ends of the K-12 system). Most recently, I've been teaching extended learning classes which I've been really enjoying!
